INKORE-BERLIN
Innovation and competence center for recycling in Berlin
master project / grand design | Großwest | Westhafen-Berlin WS 2020|21

The arrangement of the respective structures in relation to each other ensures a traffic network with direct and efficient connections.
The mixed use of mapping, research and INKORE creates a compact structure into the urban context.
The building complex is divided into three structures: THE DEPOT, the MATERIAL PREPARATION and the INKORE FORUM.
The building structures form three central courtyards that serve as a network of paths and storage space.
The development of the INKORE CENTER is at the level of the existing platform.
The INKORE FORUM appears as a two-story long building with cantilevered roof overlooking Beusselstrasse.
The new bus stop is located here and provides safe access to the building.
A generous exterior staircase is located along the west-east façade to provide a direct connection between the main entrance and the harbor level.
In addition, there is the possibility to experience the waterfront area of Berlin.
The access area of INKORE, with a two-story ceiling height and a uniform corridor width of seven and a half meters, provides a spacious exhibition area with skylight.
The concept is the merging of the INKORE forum with the research hall.
Here, the goal is to provide visitors with a tour and insight into the work processes of industrial use.
In the entrance area as well as the exhibition area, the view into the research hall is possible through glass elements in the interior walls.
The required sanitary rooms, changing rooms, showers and ancillary rooms for employees and visitors are located in this core.
Solid reinforced concrete in the base area protects the building from moisture and industrial use at port level.
In contrast to the solid base, the structure above is a timber construction strengthened by steel elements.
At an axial distance of 1.4 meters, the outwardly vertically rhythmic construction creates a uniformly articulated facade, which gives the structural building a delicate touch.
